Co-ownership exists wherever two or more people hold an estate in the same land at the same time. Since 1925 the only forms are the joint tenancy and the tenancy in common, and a legal estate can be held only on the former. Get those two sentences right and most of the topic follows.

## The structure to remember

Behind every co-owned title sits a trust of land. The legal estate is held by up to four trustees as joint tenants; the equitable interest may be held either way. Exam problems almost always turn on the equitable title, because that is where severance operates.

## The trap

Students lose marks by treating the four unities as the whole of the test, or by forgetting that severance affects only the equity. State the unities, identify the form, then ask whether anything has severed the equitable joint tenancy. Method scores; assertion does not.
