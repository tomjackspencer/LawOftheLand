The Building Safety Act 2022 was a response to a particular catastrophe, and its architecture shows it. The statute is built around the residential high-rise: the building in which people sleep, owned through leases, managed at one remove from those who live in it. That is the paradigm case, and within it the Act is coherent. The difficulty arises at the edges.

Consider the hotel. It is tall, it is occupied, and it may be clad in the same materials that prompted the legislation. But it is not let on long leases, its occupants are transient, and the relationships the Act regulates are not the relationships it contains.

## The shape of the gap

The Act defines its key terms by reference to a residential model. Where premises do not answer to that model, the draftsman's silence is not obviously a deliberate exclusion.

> A statute drawn for one class of building does not become wrong when applied to another. It becomes uncertain, which is a different and more interesting problem.

The temptation is to resolve the uncertainty by appeal to purpose. That reasoning proves too much. Purpose can identify the mischief; it cannot supply the machinery.
