An easement is a right enjoyed by one landowner over the land of another. The starting point is always the four characteristics in *Re Ellenborough Park*.

## The four characteristics

There must be a dominant and a servient tenement; the right must accommodate the dominant tenement; the two tenements must be owned or occupied by different persons; and the right must be capable of forming the subject-matter of a grant.

## The trap

Do not confuse the methods of acquisition. Keep section 62 and the rule in *Wheeldon v Burrows* distinct, and be precise about when each applies.
