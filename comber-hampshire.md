The High Court has handed down judgment in *R (Comber) v Hampshire CC* [2026] EWHC 1244 (Admin). The case concerns the registration of land as a town or village green under section 15 of the Commons Act 2006. On its facts the decision is narrow. On the role of the registration authority it is firmer.

## What the court decided

The reasoning proceeds in two steps. First, the statutory question is one of fact, and the authority is the primary finder of that fact. Second, the court's role on review is supervisory and not appellate.

> The authority's function is to determine whether the statutory conditions are met. It is not to register land because registration would be convenient, nor to refuse because refusal would be.

That sentence marks the boundary between application of a rule and exercise of a discretion.
